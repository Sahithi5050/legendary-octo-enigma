Inter-Distrct-Trackmeet
Out of 4500 students our students won a well rounded track meet.

Harborside Middle School took first place honors at the 2016 James Wynne Middle School Track Meet, held recently at Jonathan Law High School. The annual event has been in place for decades and is one of the most anticipated activities of the year at the middle school level. The annual competition is held in honor of former Milford physical education dept. chair James Wynne, who organized the first middle school track meet decades ago. Harborside Middle School tallied 189 points to put them in first place, while West Shore Middle School took second with 160 points. East Shore Middle School came in third with 145 points.

Spelling Bee
Out of the city one of our students won the city-wide spelling bee.

On Tuesday, April 30th, the Junior Woman's Club of Milford hosted the 35th Annual Citywide Spelling Bee for 5th and 6th graders at Milford City Hall. This year's Bee Master was Milford Public Library Director, Christine Angeli. Spelling Bee judges included Milford Board of Education Member, Susan Glennon and State Senator James Maroney.
